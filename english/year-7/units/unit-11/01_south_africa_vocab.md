# Vocabulary Training: Unit 11 - South Africa

## Part 1: Multicultural Society
Key terms:
1. **Apartheid:** The system of racial segregation in South Africa (1948-1994).
2. **Reconciliation:** The process of restoring friendly relations after conflict.
3. **Rainbow Nation:** Term for South Africa's diverse, multicultural society.
4. **Township:** Urban areas designated for non-white residents during apartheid.
5. **Ubuntu:** South African philosophy meaning "humanity" or "I am because we are".

## Part 2: History of South Africa
Timeline essentials:
*   **Colonial era:** European settlement and exploitation.
*   **Apartheid (1948-1994):** Systemic racial segregation and oppression.
*   **Nelson Mandela:** First black president; symbol of resistance and reconciliation.
*   **Post-apartheid:** Ongoing challenges of inequality and nation-building.

## Part 3: Literature - "Born a Crime" by Trevor Noah
Trevor Noah's memoir explores:
*   Growing up mixed-race during/after apartheid.
*   The absurdity and cruelty of racial laws.
*   His mother's strength and resilience.

## Part 4: Writing Task - Blog Post
Write a blog post (approx. 200 words) about what you learned about South Africa's journey from apartheid to democracy.
