# Vocabulary Training: Unit 12 - India

## Part 1: India - Past and Present
Key concepts:
1. **British Raj:** The period of British colonial rule in India (1858-1947).
2. **Independence movement:** Led by figures like Mahatma Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nehru.
3. **Partition (1947):** Division into India and Pakistan; caused massive displacement.
4. **Bengaluru (Bangalore):** India's "Silicon Valley" - IT and tech hub.
5. **Bollywood:** Mumbai-based Hindi-language film industry (world's largest by output).

## Part 2: Modern India
*   **Economic growth:** One of the fastest-growing major economies.
*   **Diversity:** Over 1.4 billion people; hundreds of languages.
*   **Challenges:** Poverty, inequality, environmental issues.
*   **IT sector:** Global leader in software services and outsourcing.

## Part 3: Indian Film Industry
*   **Bollywood:** Produces ~1,500 films/year.
*   **Global reach:** Indian diaspora worldwide; growing international fanbase.
*   **Themes:** Often feature music, dance, drama, and family values.

## Part 4: Writing Tasks
**Option A - Essay:** Write an essay (approx. 250 words) comparing India's colonial past with its modern tech-driven economy.

**Option B - Report:** Write a report (approx. 200-250 words) on the Indian film industry's impact on global culture.

# Grammar: Linking Words
Use: *furthermore, however, in addition, consequently, nevertheless* to connect ideas smoothly.

Example: *India has a booming tech sector; **however**, poverty remains a significant challenge.*
