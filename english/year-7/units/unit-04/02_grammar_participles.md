# Grammar Spotlight: Unit 4 - Participle Constructions

## Rule: Combining Sentences with Participles
We can use participles (e.g., *mingling, changing, bringing*) to combine sentences or provide extra info concisely.

## Exercise: Rewrite the following sentences
1.  Ben has a busy schedule. It frequently includes overseas travel.
    *   **Solution:** Ben has a busy schedule, frequently including overseas travel.
2.  A merged lifestyle can be chaotic. It mingles work and play.
    *   **Solution:** Mingling work and play, a merged lifestyle can be chaotic.
3.  Online collaboration tools make physical presence unnecessary. They make project management easier.
    *   **Solution:** Making physical presence unnecessary, online collaboration tools make project management easier.

# Professional Development: Blue, White & Pink Collar
*   **Blue-collar:** Manual workers (e.g., construction).
*   **White-collar:** Office/Management (e.g., architects, managers).
*   **Pink-collar:** Service/Care sector (e.g., nurses, sales assistants).
